Campfire Cinnamon Rolls

Prep Time: 10 Min

Cook Time: 10 Min

Total Time: 20 Min

Nothing beats waking up to the sounds of mother nature and the smell of these buttery buns!

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 3 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 pkg instant yeast
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 egg
  • Filling: 1 cup brown sugar, 1 tbsp cinnamon, 1/4 cup butter
  • Frosting: 2 tbsp butter, 4oz cream cheese, 1tsp vanilla, 2-3 cups powdered sugar

Directions

  1. Prep cinnamon rolls and frosting at home using this recipe and freeze overnight.
  2. Bake in skillet or tinfoil on the campfire, on some hot coals or with hot wood from the fire.
  3. Cook for 8-10 minutes or until golden and fully baked.
  4. Smother with icing and serve immediately.

Breakfast Hash

Prep Time: 5 Min

Cook Time: 10 Min

Total Time: 15 Min

This hearty meal tastes so good after a full day of outdoor activities.

Ingredients

  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp canola oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 large potatoes, cubed
  • 1 lb sausage or kielbasa
  • 1 can green chiles
  • 1 can whole kernel corn, drained

Directions

  1. In a large ovenproof skillet over medium heat, cook and stir onion in oil under tender. Add garlic; cook 1 minute longer. Add potatoes. Cook, uncovered, for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  2. Add kielbasa; cook and stir until meat and potatoes are tender and browned, 10-15 minutes. Stir in chiles and corn; heat through.

Cowboy Caviar

Prep Time: 15 Min

Cook Time: None

Total Time: 15 Min

A delicious bean and avocado based salad that is great for chip-dipping.

Ingredients

  • 1 can black-eyed peas
  • 1 can corn
  • 1 cup green onion, chopped
  • 1 cup cilantro, chopped
  • 1 red onion, chopped
  • 1 cup tomato, chopped
  • 2 avocados, diced
  • Lime juice, salt and pepper to taste

Directions

  1. This dish couldn't be more easy! Just combine ingredients for the dressing then mix in solid stuff. Serve with tortilla chips or on top of a bed of arugula.
  2. If you are making it a day ahead and can avoid it, don't add the tomatoes and avocados until just before you serve it. Adding a squeeze of lemon to the finished dish will keep the avocados from browning.
